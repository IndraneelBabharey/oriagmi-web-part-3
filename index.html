Camel

  1. Newborn camels don't have humps. They gradually grow as they get older.
  2. Camels are smarter than horses.
  3. Camels can live around 40 - 50 years.

Panda

  • Pandas eat almost nothing but bamboo shoots and leaves.
  • Pandas can climb as high as 13,000 feet and are also very good swimmers.
  • An adult female panda weighs 200 pounds.

Chameleon

  1. There are around 160 species of chameleon.
  2. The panther chameleon changes its color to yellow or red when it’s angry.
  3. When a chameleon is cold, it will become darker in color. Dark colors attract heat better than light ones.

Teady Bear

  • Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
  • The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.
  • The official term for a teddy bear collector is an ‘arctophile’.

Flying Cicada

  • Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs.
  • The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus.
  • Cicadas can turn themselves into an evaporative cooler.

Flying Cicada

  • Pigeons have been present with humans since ages
  • They can detect sounds at far lower frequencies than humans are able to, and can thus hear distant storms and volcanoes.
  • Pigeons can fly at altitudes up to and beyond 6000 feet, and at an average speed of 77.6 mph. The fastest recorded speed is 92.5 mph.
